Industries

Where I've secured the mission-critical.

The same discipline, proven across five very different worlds. Each one is a place where the technology cannot quietly fail.

These industries do not look alike from the outside. A core banking platform, a clinic network, a trading floor, a grocery fleet, an automated fulfillment line. What they share is the thing that shaped how I work: in each of them, the systems carry consequences that land on real people, and none of them can be paused while security catches up.

That constraint forces a particular kind of security leadership. You cannot rely on fixing things after the fact, because the safe window to touch a running system may be months out, or may never come. So the decisions that matter get made early, in how a system is segmented, how it authenticates, who can reach it, and whether security was in the room when it was designed. Every page below is a different version of that same discipline: understand how the environment actually fails, then build the guardrails upstream, before the risk is baked in.
